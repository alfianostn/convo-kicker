Deep Questions for Friends

Questions for the friends you want to know better — the ones worth going past small talk with. Ask them around a table, not over text.

  1. What kind of conversations make you feel closest to a friend?
  2. What is something people often misunderstand about you at first?
  3. What part of your life feels most important to you right now?
  4. What do you wish close friends noticed about you more often?
  5. What makes a friendship feel truly safe for you?
  6. Is there something you have been going through that you have not talked about much?
  7. What is one thing you have learned about yourself in the last year?
  8. When you are struggling, what kind of support actually helps you most?
  9. What is something you are proud of that you rarely talk about?
  10. What does loyalty mean to you in a friendship?
  11. Is there a version of yourself you are still figuring out how to become?
  12. What is the bravest thing you have done that almost no one knows about?
  13. What is a belief you hold that has changed significantly over the years?
  14. When do you feel most like yourself?
  15. What is something you are still learning how to ask for?
  16. What is a part of your story that shaped who you are but rarely comes up?
  17. What does it mean to you to feel truly understood by someone?
  18. What is one thing you wish you gave yourself more credit for?
  19. Is there a dream you have quietly held onto for a long time?
  20. What would you want a close friend to know about what you are carrying right now?
